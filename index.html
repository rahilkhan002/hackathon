Domestic waste management refers to the process of collecting, transporting, processing, recycling, and disposing of waste generated by households. It's a crucial aspect of urban and rural sanitation to maintain cleanliness, prevent pollution, and promote public health.

Important steps that we have to take ragarding Waste Management

Composting Programs

Encourage composting of organic waste at the household level. Provide composting bins or offer composting workshops to teach residents how to compost their food scraps and yard waste.

Assessment and Planning

Conduct a thorough assessment of the current waste management system to identify major issues and areas for improvement. This may involve analyzing waste generation patterns, collection methods, disposal practices, infrastructure deficiencies, and regulatory gaps. Develop a strategic plan with clear goals, targets, and timelines for addressing these issues.

Public Awareness and Education

Increase public awareness and education about proper waste disposal practices, recycling, and the importance of reducing waste generation. Implement educational campaigns, workshops, and outreach programs targeting households, schools, and communities to promote behavior change and responsible waste management habits.
